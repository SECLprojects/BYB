# Bring Your Bills — guide for interested parties

For funders, councils, researchers, journalists, community organisations
and anyone else who wants to understand what Bring Your Bills is and see
it in action.

## What Bring Your Bills is

Bring Your Bills (BYB) is a program of **South East Community Links
(SECL)**, funded by the **Victorian Government**. Community members walk
into a free local event with their bills — energy, water, phone,
internet — and get one-on-one help on the spot: understanding the bill,
accessing concessions and utility relief grants, negotiating payment
plans, and connecting with other support services. Free, walk-in, no
appointment, interpreters available.

## Seeing it for yourself — the website

Type this into your browser:

> **bringyourbills.org.au**

(`bringyourbills.com.au` and `bringyourbills.au` reach the same site.)

| Page | Address | What you'll see |
|---|---|---|
| Home | `bringyourbills.org.au` | The next event, what people should bring, what happens on the day |
| Calendar | `bringyourbills.org.au/calendar.html` | Every event, month by month, with a "find events near you" postcode search and region filters |
| Map | `bringyourbills.org.au/map.html` | Every upcoming event as a pin across Victoria |
| Event pages | linked from the calendar and map | One page per event: full details plus the services attending, with their logos |

Events are colour-coded by Victorian region (following the DFFH-style
catchment groupings) and flagged by status (open / full / call to
discuss).

## How events get onto the calendar

- **SECL** adds and manages its own events directly.
- **Partner organisations** (services, councils) submit events through a
  passcode-protected request form; **SECL staff review and approve**
  every partner submission before it goes live. Partners can also list
  their organisation's logo and confirm attendance at each other's
  events — which is how each event page builds its "services at this
  event" list.

## Privacy, in brief

The site is deliberately light-touch: no accounts, no cookies-based
analytics, no third-party trackers. Community members can *optionally*
say they're coming to an event — every personal field is optional, the
data is never public, it's only used to plan staffing and interpreters,
and it's automatically deleted after 5 years. The site aligns with the
Victorian Information Privacy Principles; SECL's full privacy policy is
at **secl.org.au/policies**.

## Want to get involved?

- **Run or host an event with SECL, or have your service attend one** —
  email **byb@secl.org.au** and ask about becoming a partner (you'll be
  given the partner passcode and the [providers & partners
  guide](providers-partners.md)).
- **Refer community members** — just point them at
  **bringyourbills.org.au**, or the [community members
  guide](community-members.md). No referral process needed; everything
  is free and walk-in.
- **Media, research, funding enquiries** — **byb@secl.org.au**, or SECL
  generally at **secl.org.au**.
